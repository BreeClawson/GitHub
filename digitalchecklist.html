Your Digital Infrastructure
Checklist

Everything a small business owner needs to build a solid digital foundation — before spending a dollar on ads.

Free Resource

Before you grow, you need a strong foundation. This checklist covers the six pillars every small business needs to show up professionally online, attract the right clients, and convert visitors into leads. Work through each section and check off what you have — then use the gaps as your action plan.

🌐
Section 01
Website Essentials
  • Professional domain namee.g. yourbusiness.com — not a free subdomain
  • SSL certificate (HTTPS)The padlock icon in the browser — required for trust & Google ranking
  • Clear homepage messageVisitors should know what you do and who you serve in 5 seconds
  • Contact form or booking linkMake it easy for leads to reach you without hunting
  • Mobile-friendly designOver 60% of web traffic is on phones — test yours!
  • Fast load timePages should load in under 3 seconds or visitors leave
Pro Tip: Test your site speed for free at pagespeed.web.dev — Google uses this to rank your site.
📱
Section 02
Social Media Setup
  • Consistent username across platformsSame handle on Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn
  • Professional profile photoClear headshot or branded logo — no blurry selfies
  • Complete bio with keywordsWho you help, what you do, and a call to action
  • Link in bio points to your websiteOr a Linktree with your key pages
  • Branded cover photoFacebook and LinkedIn banners should reflect your brand
  • Pinned post or highlight reelFirst thing people see should showcase your best work
Pro Tip: You don't need to be on every platform. Pick 2 where your ideal clients spend time and show up consistently there.
📧
Section 03
Email & Communication
  • Professional email addressyou@yourbusiness.com — not gmail or yahoo
  • Email signature with linksName, title, website, social handles
  • Auto-reply or out-of-office set upLet people know when to expect a response
  • Email marketing platform connectedMailerLite, Mailchimp, or Flodesk to build your list
  • Welcome email sequenceAt least 1 email that goes out when someone subscribes
Pro Tip: Your email list is the only audience you truly own. Social media can disappear overnight — your list is yours forever.
📅
Section 04
Content Planning
  • Defined content pillars (3–5 topics)What themes will you consistently post about?
  • Posting scheduleEven 2x per week is better than sporadic bursts
  • Content bank (10+ ideas ready)So you never stare at a blank screen wondering what to post
  • Brand templates in CanvaConsistent colors, fonts, and layouts for every post
  • Monthly content calendarPlan at least 2 weeks ahead to reduce stress
Pro Tip: Batch your content creation — set aside 2 hours once a week to create a week's worth of posts at once.
🛠️
Section 05
Tools & Tech Stack
  • Project management toolNotion, Trello, or Asana to track tasks and client work
  • Scheduling tool for social mediaBuffer, Later, or Meta Business Suite
  • Online booking or scheduling linkCalendly or Google Calendar appointment booking
  • CRM or client trackerEven a simple spreadsheet to track leads and clients
  • Cloud storage for assetsGoogle Drive or Dropbox for logos, photos, documents
  • Analytics trackingGoogle Analytics on your website to track visitors
Pro Tip: Start simple. You don't need every tool — pick one for each category and master it before adding more.
📍
Section 06
Visibility & Local Presence
  • Google Business Profile claimed & completeFree listing that shows up in Google Maps and local search
  • Business listed in local directoriesYelp, Chamber of Commerce, industry directories
  • Reviews actively requestedAsk happy clients for Google or Facebook reviews
  • SEO basics on your websitePage titles, descriptions, and keywords that match what clients search
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone)Same info everywhere online — Google uses this for ranking
Pro Tip: Google Business Profile is one of the most powerful free tools available. If you haven't claimed yours yet, do it today at business.google.com.
